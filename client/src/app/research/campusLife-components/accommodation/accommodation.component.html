Overview

College accommodation provides students with a conducive living environment, fostering academic success and personal growth. Typically, colleges offer various housing options, including dormitories, apartments, or shared housing. On-campus housing provides proximity to academic facilities, promoting a sense of community and facilitating participation in campus activities. It encourages social interactions, creating a supportive network among students. Additionally, residential life often includes resident advisors, enhancing the overall college experience by offering guidance and organizing community-building events. Off-campus housing may offer greater independence but requires careful consideration of location and amenities. Overall, college accommodation plays a vital role in shaping students' collegiate journey.

Vision

  • Create welcoming spaces ensuring students feel at home in accommodation.

  • Offer secure, friendly, comfortable, well-furnished ambiance for student accommodation satisfaction.

  • Cultivate diverse, competitive, healthy environment for cultural learning in accommodation.

  • Instill discipline, foster systematic and strategic mindset for student development.

  • Offer resources and facilities conducive to academic success, including study areas, computer labs, and access to educational materials.

Do's & Don'ts

Do's

  • Familiarize yourself with the hostel rules and regulations.
  • Keep your room clean and tidy to maintain a conducive living environment.
  • Respect the privacy and belongings of your roommates and fellow residents.
  • Follow the designated meal times and dining hall etiquette.
  • Report any maintenance issues or concerns to the hostel authorities promptly.
  • Participate in hostel activities and community events to build friendships and a sense of belonging.
  • Keep track of important dates and deadlines related to hostel fees, renewals, and check-out procedures.
  • Follow safety protocols and emergency procedures outlined by the hostel administration.

Don'ts

  • Don't violate hostel rules or engage in behavior that disrupts the peace and harmony of the hostel.
  • Don't disturb other residents with loud noises or disruptive activities, especially during quiet hours.
  • Don't tamper with or damage hostel property, including furniture, fixtures, and equipment.
  • Don't bring unauthorized guests into the hostel without prior permission from the hostel authorities.
  • Don't indulge in illegal activities or substance abuse within the hostel premises.
  • Don't leave valuables or personal belongings unattended or unlocked in common areas.
  • Don't engage in bullying, harassment, or discriminatory behavior towards other residents.
  • Don't ignore safety protocols or fail to report any safety hazards or security concerns to the hostel authorities promptly.
