Overview

The Phytosanitary Laboratory at IGKV serves as a vital hub for testing various agricultural samples, catering to the diverse needs of government agencies, farmers, researchers, academic institutions, private industries, and individuals. Equipped with state-of-the-art equipment and skilled technicians, the laboratory conducts comprehensive analyses of soil, water, crop residue, microbial broth, cereals, vegetables, fruits, and aromatic plants. Through meticulous testing procedures, it ensures compliance with phytosanitary standards, identifying potential threats such as pests, diseases, and contaminants. By providing accurate and timely results, the laboratory facilitates informed decision-making in crop management, pest control, and food safety. Its services play a crucial role in safeguarding agricultural productivity, environmental sustainability, and public health. Additionally, the laboratory serves as a valuable resource for research and innovation, fostering collaborations between academia, industry, and government entities to address emerging challenges in agriculture and promote sustainable development.

Facilities

Testing for Liquid Chromatography

  • Liquid Chromatography (LC) testing is a versatile analytical technique used to separate, identify, and quantify components in a liquid sample.
  • It involves passing a liquid sample through a column packed with a stationary phase, where components interact differently based on their chemical properties.
  • LC can analyze a wide range of compounds, including organic molecules, pharmaceuticals, environmental pollutants, and biomolecules.
  • It provides high sensitivity and selectivity, making it suitable for complex mixtures and trace-level analysis.
  • LC testing is utilized in various industries, including pharmaceuticals, environmental monitoring, food and beverage, and forensic science.
  • It enables researchers to characterize and quantify compounds accurately, aiding in quality control, research, and regulatory compliance.

Gas Chromatography

  • Gas Chromatography (GC) is an analytical technique used to separate and analyze volatile compounds in a gas sample.
  • It involves injecting the sample into a column packed with a stationary phase, where components are separated based on their affinity for the stationary phase and the mobile phase (carrier gas).
  • GC is highly sensitive and offers excellent resolution, making it suitable for analyzing complex mixtures and trace-level compounds.
  • It is widely used in various industries, including pharmaceuticals, environmental analysis, food and beverage, petrochemicals, and forensics.
  • GC enables rapid analysis, precise quantification, and identification of compounds, aiding in research, quality control, and regulatory compliance.
  • Different types of detectors, such as flame ionization detector (FID), electron capture detector (ECD), and mass spectrometry (MS), can be used with GC to enhance detection capabilities and specificity.

Mass Spectrometer Testing

  • Mass Spectrometry (MS) testing is an analytical technique used to identify and quantify compounds based on their mass-to-charge ratio.
  • It involves ionizing the sample, separating ions based on their mass-to-charge ratio, and detecting and quantifying ions to determine the molecular composition.
  • MS provides high sensitivity, accuracy, and specificity, making it suitable for analyzing complex mixtures and trace-level compounds.
  • It is widely used in various fields, including pharmaceuticals, environmental analysis, food safety, forensics, and proteomics.
  • MS enables the identification of unknown compounds, characterization of molecular structures, and quantification of analytes with high precision.
  • Different MS techniques, such as liquid chromatography-mass spectrometry (LC-MS) and gas chromatography-mass spectrometry (GC-MS), offer versatility in analytical testing.
